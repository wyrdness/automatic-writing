## Overview

Automatic writing (also psychography or trance writing) is the practice of **writing without conscious thought**, supposedly allowing spirits, subconscious minds, or other sources to communicate through a person's hand. The writer typically enters a trance or dissociative state, allowing the hand to move across paper without deliberate control, producing written messages.

The phenomenon gained prominence during the **Spiritualist movement** (19th-20th century), when mediums used automatic writing to channel messages from the dead. Modern practitioners include **psychics, New Age adherents, and experimental psychologists**. Some writers report accessing creativity through automatic writing, while skeptics attribute it to the **ideomotor effect**—unconscious muscle movements.

Automatic writing exists at the intersection of **spiritualism, psychology, creativity, and potential fraud**. While some cases clearly involve conscious deception, others represent genuine dissociative states or subconscious expression. The technique continues in modern spiritualist, occult, and therapeutic contexts.

## Physical Description

Automatic writing involves specific physical manifestations:

**The Process**
- **Hand Movement**: Writer's hand moves without conscious direction
- **Speed**: Often faster than normal writing
- **Style**: May differ dramatically from writer's normal handwriting
- **Posture**: Writer may adopt unusual positions
- **Eyes**: Sometimes closed or unfocused
- **Trance State**: Varying degrees of consciousness

**Written Output**
- **Content**: May be unknown to conscious mind
- **Languages**: Rarely, claimed foreign or unknown languages (xenoglossy claims)
- **Handwriting**: Different styles, sometimes multiple distinct "hands"
- **Coherence**: Ranges from nonsensical to remarkably coherent
- **Artistic Elements**: Sometimes includes drawings or symbols
- **Speed**: Rapid, flowing writing without pauses

**Physiological Signs**
- **Dissociation**: Writer appears in trance
- **Autonomic Changes**: Altered breathing, pupil dilation
- **Memory**: Often poor recall of what was written
- **Physical Sensation**: Hand moving "on its own"
- **Fatigue**: Sessions may be exhausting
